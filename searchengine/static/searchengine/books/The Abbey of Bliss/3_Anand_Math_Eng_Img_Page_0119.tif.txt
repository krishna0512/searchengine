CHAPTER IV
 After Finishing his evening prayers, Satyananda called
 Mahendra and said,
 &lsquo;&ldquo;Your daughter still lives.&rsquo;&rsquo;
 &ldquo;Where, Maharaj ?"
 &lsquo;'Why do yon call me &lsquo;Maharaj&rsquo; ?&rdquo;
 &ldquo;Because every body calls you so and I think heads
 of monasteries are so called everywhere. Where is my
 daughter, Maharaj ?&rdquo;
 &lsquo;'Before you hear that, give me a clear and definite
 answer as to your wishes to take the vow of The
children ?
 &lsquo;'I have made up my mind to take it.&rsquo;&rdquo;
"Then do not want to learn where your daughter is.&rdquo;
 &lsquo;&lsquo;Why, Maharaj ?&rdquo;
" When any one takes this vow, he has to snap all
 ties that bind him to his wife, children and kinsmen. You
 have got to expiate even the transgression of seeing the
 face of your wife or children. Till the Children have
 reached the consummation of their desire, you may not
 even look at your daughter&rsquo;s face. So that, if you have
 decided to take the vow of &lsquo;The Children, what will you
 do with knowing where your daughter is ? You cannot
 see her by any means now.&rsquo;&rdquo;
